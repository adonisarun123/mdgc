## 1. Club History (source: `/history`)

Page heading: **"HISTORY OF MDGC"**

Verbatim passages:

> "a group of Englishmen clear-felled the area which was destined to become the Mercara Downs Golf Club" — describing the early 1890s origin.

> By the turn of the century the course attracted increasing patronage. World War I brought military officers who boosted its popularity, and by WWII "one could see a sprinkling of Indians, both civilians, and officers alike, on the course."

> Following WWII: "the exodus of British officers, Independent India came into being and the archives lost its most valuable records."

> In 1951 the club became "incorporated as an extension of the North Coorg Club", operating independently only from "the early 80s."

> Present day: "18 hole Golf Course spread over 100 acres" with a clubhouse and "eight chambers called 'Mercara Down's Retreat.'"

> Affiliations: "65 clubs in India" plus Nuwara Eliya (Sri Lanka) and Noosa Springs (Australia).

Note: a second, possibly longer history narrative exists on `/mdgc-history` but could not be extracted remotely (client-rendered/image content). Capture manually before rewrite.

Editorial guidance already agreed: do not claim a precise founding year; use "Golf has been played on the Mercara Downs since the late nineteenth century" phrasing until the club documents the 1890s claim.

## 3. The 18 Holes (source: `/course-layout`)

Hole names as published, with playing notes. Direct quotes are marked; unquoted phrasing is *(condensed)* from the live text and should be re-captured verbatim in a manual pass.

1. **Fort Knox** — Par 4. "A difficult opening hole that looks innocuous off the tee" — uphill second shot to a small, fast green protected by two back bunkers.
2. **Downs View** — Par 3. All downhill, 130–160 yards depending on wind; a "D" shaped green surrounded by four bunkers.
3. **Blinder** — Dogleg-left Par 4. A road crossing is in play off the tee; second shot to a small green with two right-side bunkers; steep back-to-front slope on the green.
4. **Upper Crest** — Par 3. "All uphill from the Tee"; bunkers on both sides; the green is set back so the flag is not visible from the tee.
5. **Leveller** — Par 5. Wide fairway from an elevated tee; "Reachable in 2 for the long hitters"; shallow front-left bunker.
6. **Misty** — Par 3. Downhill to a green that is "Quite large"; avoid going long; "Aim for the center of the green as its where the green is deepest." *(sic — "its")*
7. **Lapwing Nest** — Dogleg-left Par 4. Long hole, uphill then downslope; severe drop-off behind the green; bunkers front-left and back-right.
8. **The Pride** — Par 3, 197 yards, "rated the 4th toughest hole on the course"; water hazard; elevated tee shot across a valley.
9. **Runway** — Dogleg-left Par 4. Long; fairway slopes downhill after 250 yards; "Do NOT want to belong on this hole." *(sic — presumably "be long")*
10. **Power Play** — Par 4. Green is a blind spot from the tee; "Be aggressive off the tee & go for distance"; elevated green falls away right.
11. **Rush Hour** — Dogleg-left Par 4. Elevated tee; trees border the left; a "good birdie opportunity" for long hitters going for the green in two.
12. **Captain's Hole** — Par 4. Short; slopes left to right; "green falls away on the right"; elevated, narrow green.
13. **Un Macho** — Par 5. Long and straight; landing area blind from the tee; "Play for par on this hole & you could end up with a birdie."
14. **Deceptive** — Par 3. Short; forest (out of bounds) right; bunker left.
15. **Boomerang** — Dogleg-left Par 5. Sharp dogleg; "fairway slopes sharply from right to left"; elevated green with front-left bunker.
16. **Waterloo** — Par 3. Very short; green about 30 feet below the tee; water hazard right; "very easy Par 3" with a bailout left.
17. **Guts & Glory** — Par 4, 250 yards; two water bodies to carry; "This is very much a birdie hole."
18. **Pressure** — Par 3. Water carry of 120 yards; out of bounds left at 130 yards; tall tree left; two bunkers; "tough green to put on." *(sic — "putt")*

Implied par check: pars as listed sum to 4+3+4+3+5+3+4+3+4 (front 33) + 4+4+4+5+3+5+3+4+3 (back 35) = **68, which contradicts the published par 70**. Either some listed pars were mis-captured or the page itself is inconsistent — resolve against the official scorecard. Hole names, spelling, capitalisation and apostrophes (Captain's Hole, Guts & Glory, Un Macho) all need formal club confirmation.

Signature-hole candidates flagged in the brief (Fort Knox, Misty, The Pride, Waterloo, Guts & Glory, Pressure) all have usable legacy copy above.
